# REQ-o80010: Training and Personnel Qualification

**Level**: Ops | **Status**: Draft | **Implements**: p00002, p00003, p00004, p00005

## Rationale

Personnel who use electronic systems for regulated activities must be trained and qualified. This requirement establishes training and qualification obligations for system users.

## Assertions

A. The organization SHALL establish education, training, and experience requirements for personnel who develop, maintain, or use electronic record/electronic signature systems.
   *Source: 21 CFR Part 11 (up to date as of 1-22-2026).pdf, 11.10(i)*

B. The organization SHALL document training completion for each user before granting system access for regulated activities.
   *Source: GCP Data - Consolidated Requirements for Audit Trails & Data Corrections - 02Sept2025.pdf, Req 18*

C. The organization SHALL provide training on data integrity principles, including ALCOA++ concepts, for all users who create or modify clinical data.
   *Source: GCP Data - Requirements for Audit Trails & Data Corrections - 02Sept2025.pdf, Training requirements*

D. The organization SHALL ensure training is role-appropriate and covers the specific system functions each role may access.
   *Source: 21 CFR Part 11 (up to date as of 1-22-2026).pdf, 11.10(i)*

E. The organization SHALL ensure personnel are qualified by education, training, and experience for their delegated trial-related duties.
   *Source: GCP Data - Requirements for Audit Trails & Data Corrections - 02Sept2025.pdf, ICH E6(R3) 4.2*

F. The organization SHALL maintain training records for the duration of employment plus the required record retention period.
   *Source: GCP Data - Consolidated Requirements for Audit Trails & Data Corrections - 02Sept2025.pdf, Req 18*

G. The organization SHALL require retraining when significant system changes are implemented.
   *Source: GCP Data - Consolidated Requirements for Audit Trails & Data Corrections - 02Sept2025.pdf, Req 29*

*End* *Training and Personnel Qualification* | **Hash**: 53e1ba24

# REQ-o80020: Record Retention and Archival

**Level**: Ops | **Status**: Draft | **Implements**: p00002, p00003, p00005

## Rationale

Electronic records and their associated metadata must be retained and accessible for the required retention periods. This requirement establishes retention and archival obligations.

## Assertions

A. The organization SHALL protect records to enable their accurate and ready retrieval throughout the records retention period.
   *Source: 21 CFR Part 11 (up to date as of 1-22-2026).pdf, 11.10(c)*

B. The organization SHALL retain electronic records in accordance with FDA guidance for clinical investigations.
   *Source: 2024 10 FDA Guidance on electronic storaage 0 58358119fnl.pdf, Section II*

C. The organization SHALL ensure records remain accessible and readable throughout the retention period, including through technology migrations.
   *Source: 21 CFR Part 11 (up to date as of 1-22-2026).pdf, 11.10(c)*

D. The organization SHALL ensure long-term accessibility and readability of electronic records.
   *Source: GCP Data - Consolidated Requirements for Audit Trails & Data Corrections - 02Sept2025.pdf, Req 26*

E. The organization SHALL implement periodic verification of archived record integrity and accessibility.
   *Source: GCP Data - Consolidated Requirements for Audit Trails & Data Corrections - 02Sept2025.pdf, Req 27*

F. The organization SHALL maintain the ability to produce records in formats suitable for regulatory inspection.
   *Source: 21 CFR Part 11 (up to date as of 1-22-2026).pdf, 11.10(b)*

G. The organization SHALL document and test recovery procedures for archived electronic records.
   *Source: GCP Data - Consolidated Requirements for Audit Trails & Data Corrections - 02Sept2025.pdf, Req 27*

H. The organization SHALL ensure audit trail data is retained for at least as long as the associated electronic records.
   *Source: 21 CFR Part 11 (up to date as of 1-22-2026).pdf, 11.10(e)*

# REQ-o80030: Standard Operating Procedures

**Level**: Ops | **Status**: Draft | **Implements**: p00002, p00003, p00005

## Rationale

Documented procedures are essential for consistent operation of electronic systems and compliance with regulatory requirements. This requirement establishes SOP obligations.

## Assertions

A. The organization SHALL maintain written policies that hold individuals accountable for actions initiated under their electronic signatures.
   *Source: 21 CFR Part 11 (up to date as of 1-22-2026).pdf, 11.10(j)*

B. The organization SHALL limit system access to authorized individuals through documented procedures.
   *Source: 21 CFR Part 11 (up to date as of 1-22-2026).pdf, 11.10(d)*

C. The organization SHALL document procedures for granting, modifying, and revoking system access.
   *Source: GCP Data - Consolidated Requirements for Audit Trails & Data Corrections - 02Sept2025.pdf, Req 19*

D. The organization SHALL document procedures for responding to security incidents including unauthorized access attempts.
   *Source: GCP Data - Consolidated Requirements for Audit Trails & Data Corrections - 02Sept2025.pdf, Req 25*

E. The organization SHALL document procedures for backup, recovery, and business continuity.
   *Source: GCP Data - Consolidated Requirements for Audit Trails & Data Corrections - 02Sept2025.pdf, Req 27*

F. The organization SHALL document procedures for system change control and configuration management.
   *Source: GCP Data - Consolidated Requirements for Audit Trails & Data Corrections - 02Sept2025.pdf, Req 29*

G. The organization SHALL maintain distribution, access, and use controls over systems documentation.
   *Source: 21 CFR Part 11 (up to date as of 1-22-2026).pdf, 11.10(k)*

*End* *Standard Operating Procedures* | **Hash**: 50d11093
